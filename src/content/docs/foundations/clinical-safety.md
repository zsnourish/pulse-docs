Pulse advocates for safe, effective clinical care through thoughtful design. This guidance has been outlined with our Clinical team, to enable users to make the right decisions, at the right time, for the right patient, whilst reducing the likelihood of error, ambiguity, or missed information especially in pressured clinical contexts.

## Design Principles for Clinical Safety

Each area below represents a lens through which design decisions should be viewed:

1. Ensure users **always know whose record** they are viewing or editing. Consider how person context persists across screens, complex workflows, or interruptions.
2. Design interfaces that **guide users toward safe actions** and prevent inappropriate ones. Make constraints clear through contextual cues, not memory.
3. Support clear understanding of **what needs doing, by whom, and when.** Make states (complete, pending, required) obvious to prevent ambiguity.
4. **Minimise risks** from incorrect, incomplete, or conflicting data. Use validation, feedback, and layout to support accuracy.
5. **Highlight time‑sensitive or clinically important data** without overwhelming the user. Balance prominence with cognitive load.
6. **Assume clinical environments are pressured** and distraction‑rich. Keep interactions simple, language clear, and layouts supportive of quick, safe decisions.
7. Enable users to **verify, undo, and audit actions.** Make system states traceable and reversible where appropriate.
8. Design for **variations in ability, context, and access needs.** Avoid relying on a single sensory cue, and ensure inclusivity supports safety for all users.
