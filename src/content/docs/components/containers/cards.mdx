**Card components** are containers for sets of related information and actions, and can also contain panels e.g. for lists. Cards can be static, or interactive and clickable.

### Usage & Content Guidelines

<div class="ds-callout ds-do">
<p><strong>✓ Do</strong> — Use to group related information (e.g., a product, user profile, or article preview).</p>
</div>
<div class="ds-callout ds-do">
<p><strong>✓ Do</strong> — Ensure actions are clearly scoped to the card's content.</p>
</div>
<div class="ds-callout ds-dont">
<p><strong>✗ Don't</strong> — Avoid overloading with too much information or too many actions.</p>
</div>
<div class="ds-callout ds-dont">
<p><strong>✗ Don't</strong> — Do not use for primary page structure; cards live inside the main page container.</p>
</div>

## Empty States

Card component variant used when there is no data to display, providing context and a guided action to the user. Includes an icon or illustration, a short message and where possible, a primary action button (e.g., "Add Item") to guide the user's journey.

### Usage & Content Guidelines

<div class="ds-callout ds-do">
<p><strong>✓ Do</strong> — Use to clearly explain why the card is empty and what the user can do next.</p>
</div>
<div class="ds-callout ds-do">
<p><strong>✓ Do</strong> — Provide a clear call-to-action that directly addresses the empty state.</p>
</div>
<div class="ds-callout ds-dont">
<p><strong>✗ Don't</strong> — Do not leave the card blank or display an error message.</p>
</div>
<div class="ds-callout ds-dont">
<p><strong>✗ Don't</strong> — Do not use for system errors; this is for neutral, user-resolvable states.</p>
</div>
